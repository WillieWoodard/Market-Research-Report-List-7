<p><p>The microemulsions market includes several types based on the nature of surfactants used. Anionic microemulsions utilize negatively charged surfactants, enhancing stability and solubilization in various applications. Non-ionic microemulsions, characterized by neutral surfactants, offer versatility and reduced toxicity, making them suitable for pharmaceuticals and cosmetics. Cationic microemulsions employ positively charged surfactants, providing antibacterial properties and improved moisture retention, ideal for hair care and skin products. Zwitterionic microemulsions contain both positive and negative charges, allowing enhanced compatibility with diverse formulations.</p></p>
