<p><ul><li>Hardware-based AV-over-IP</li><li>Software-based AV-over-IP</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The AV-over-IP market is divided into two main types: hardware-based and software-based solutions. Hardware-based AV-over-IP utilizes dedicated physical devices, such as encoders and decoders, to manage audio and video signals over IP networks. This approach often ensures lower latency and higher reliability. In contrast, software-based AV-over-IP relies on applications and virtual systems running on general-purpose computing hardware. This offers greater flexibility and scalability but may involve more complexity and higher latency compared to hardware solutions.</p></p>
