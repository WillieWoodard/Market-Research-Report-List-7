<p><p>The Taurine market is primarily segmented based on production methods: the Ethylene Oxide Method and the Ethanol Amine Method. The Ethylene Oxide Method involves the reaction of taurine precursors with ethylene oxide, resulting in high purity taurine commonly used in food and pharmaceutical sectors. In contrast, the Ethanol Amine Method employs ethanolamine as a reactant, offering an alternative production route. Both methods cater to diverse applications, including dietary supplements and animal feed, influencing market dynamics and pricing strategies.</p></p>
