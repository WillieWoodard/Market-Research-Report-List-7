<p><p>The bio-based phenol market encompasses various sources used for production. Lignin source phenols are derived from lignin, a complex organic polymer found in plant cell walls, offering a sustainable alternative to traditional phenol. Cellulose source phenols are obtained from cellulose, the primary structural component of plants, while hemicellulose/xylan source phenols come from hemicellulose, a complex carbohydrate in plant materials. Other sources may include agricultural residues or waste streams, further diversifying the market and promoting eco-friendly phenol production methods.</p></p>
