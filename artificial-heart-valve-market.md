<p><p>An artificial heart valve is a medical device that replaces damaged or malfunctioning heart valves in patients with valvular heart disease. <p><p>The artificial heart valve market comprises three primary types: </p><p>1. **Transcatheter Heart Valves** - These minimally invasive valves are implanted via catheterization, offering quicker recovery and reduced surgical risks.</p><p>2. **Tissue Heart Valves** - Made from animal or human tissue, these valves are biocompatible and provide good hemodynamic performance, but may require replacement after a decade.</p><p>3. **Mechanical Heart Valves** - Constructed from durable materials, these valves have a longer lifespan but may increase the risk of blood clots, necessitating lifelong anticoagulation therapy.</p></p>
