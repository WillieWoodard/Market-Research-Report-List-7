<p><ul><li>Iodine-123</li><li>Echnetium-99m</li><li>Xenon-133</li><li>Thallium-201</li><li>Fluorine-18</li></ul></p>
<p><p>SPECT scanning devices utilize various radioisotopes for diagnostic imaging in nuclear medicine. Iodine-123 is commonly used for thyroid and brain imaging, while Technetium-99m is the most prevalent, ideal for various organ studies due to its short half-life and gamma emission. Xenon-133 is employed for lung ventilation imaging, Thallium-201 is primarily used in cardiac studies, and Fluorine-18, although more associated with PET scans, also finds applications in certain SPECT imaging contexts. These isotopes enhance the precision and effectiveness of nuclear imaging techniques.</p></p>
