<p><p>Immersion cooling is a method used to remove heat from electronic components, primarily in data centers and mining operations. The market is segmented into liquid cooling and air cooling types. Liquid cooling involves submerging components in a thermally conductive liquid, enhancing heat dissipation and efficiency. In contrast, air cooling relies on air circulation to manage temperature. While liquid cooling offers superior thermal management and energy efficiency, air cooling is more straightforward and cost-effective for less intensive applications.</p></p>
