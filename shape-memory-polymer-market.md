<p><p>The Shape-Memory Polymer market is categorized into several types based on the stimuli that trigger their shape recovery. Temperature-induced polymers respond to thermal changes, reverting to a predefined shape when heated. Light-induced polymers utilize ultraviolet or visible light for activation, enabling precise control. Electricity-induced types rely on electrical currents to initiate shape changes. Additionally, other stimulus-responsive categories include pH-sensitive and magnetic-responsive polymers, which react to environmental changes in acidity and magnetic fields, offering diverse applications across various industries.</p></p>
